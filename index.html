Week 3: 9/08-9/18 - On the 8th, we started building the Rube Goldberg Machine. We measured and cut our elements out of cardboad and taped some of the pieces together. We have had to make some modifications to our design to help with stability and solve some probelms that came up. We finished our Rube Goldberg Machine on Monday the 18th and took a video of it working. There is a link to the video below along with pictures of the final product and the process of making the Rube Goldberg Machine. There were many problems we had to fix along the way and, although frustrating, seeing it finally work was really rewarding. We determined, to save materials and space, that we didn't need the lever to be standing on a box. So, we did not make a box and, instead, shortened the sides of the overhead cardboard piece and taped the lever to the base. Originally, we wanted to use a counter weight for the pulley and just let go of it to let the baseball down. However, the baseball was hitting the lever too hard and fast, causing the car to flip over instead of rolling down the ramp. We added more weight, but then the baseball wasn't heavy enough to pull it. We tried many different weights and even tried wrapping the string around a pencil so it would unravel. However, we just ended up tieing an eraser to the string and flinging the eraser to release the tension on the string and let the baseball down. We did some tests without the balloon and determined it would work. Then, we got the balloon and recorded our machine in action.

Week 5: 10/02-10/06 - After finishing our Computer Engineering unit, we moved on the Bioengineering. We had to make a prosthetic hand that could pick up an empty, plastic water bottle. We worked in groups of two or three, working on the hand for almost an entire period. Me and my partner originally cut out an oversized hand from carboard and tried to start barinstorming. We had the fingers spread out, but determined it would be better to have them all line up. So, we used the first cutout as a test subject. We tried threading the string through holes in the fingers, but we couldn't get it to fold right. We taped the string to the tip of the finger and tried to figure out how to secure the rest of the tape along the finger. We knew that tapeing the string down would probably not work as when we pulled the string it would just break the cardboard. We decided on taping the string down, but putting a smaller piece of tape over part of the sticky part where the string would rest. So, the string had something to slide on so when we pulled the string it would bunch up and bend the finger. We cut slits in the fingers to make them bend better and taped the middle and ring finger together, as we had originally made them too thin. We made individual strings for each finger and taped them together at the bottom. We tested it out, but it was awkward to just hold the had. So we added a cardboard arm and it ended up being able to pick up the water bottle. There are pictures of our design and a video of it working below.

Week 6: 10/09-10/20 - During this time, we have been doing our Aerospace Engineering unit. We learned what Aerospace Engineering was and learned about the four forces on airplanes and rockets. We used this knowledge on weight, thrust, lift, and drag for our project. We had to create a 2-liter bottle rocket that would be powered by water and air pressure. We did a CAD of our design and then got to building. We attached a pencil to the side of the bottle to slide into the "rails" on the launchpad that would guide the rocket. Below is a picture of our CAD, our finished design, and a video of our rocket in action. Our rocket spun around in a circle while in the air, as did many other projects. I think the weight was not distributed correctly. For our project, we originally made the four fins touching the ground so the rocket could stand up by itself. We realized the neck of the bottle could no longer fit into the launchpad, so we had to cut down the fins. We put a paper cone on the end of the bottle and put a paper ball inside the cone so it would be more stable. We used paper instead of cardboard as we thought cardboard would make the front too heavy.
